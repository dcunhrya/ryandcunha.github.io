Analog hearing aid with operational-amplifiers and filters for speech amplification with ambient noise dampening

  • Skills: Circuit Design, Filters

Project Goals

    • Create low-cost hearing aid out of analog components
    • Amplify and filter two passbands common for hearing loss
    • Perform ANSI and FDA testing and analysis on device

Circuit Design

Circuit designed in KiCad with main functionality filtering two passbands, amplifying, summing togther, and passing to audio amplifier. Input from microphone powered by RC circuit. Output passed to barrel jack converter to headphones.

Hardware Components

Operational-amplifiers (LF353, LM386), Microphone (CMEJ), Headphone Jack (STX-3500), Decoupling Capacitors, RC Circuits

Frequency Filtering

Two passbands at 100-1845Hz and 2250-7500Hz to obtain fundamental frequencies, vowels, and fricative consonants which are most common during hearing loss. Passed tests for frequency matching, frequency response, and total harmonic distortion

Gain Amplification

Gain of total hearing aid was 38 or 60 depending on passband with lower frequency having lower gain. Pass tests for signal-to-noise ratio, speech intelligibility, and gain response
