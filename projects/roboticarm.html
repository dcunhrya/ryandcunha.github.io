    Algorithm Design

    A deep 3-layer neural network was created to classify the arm angles for regression. The hidden layers are of size 64, 32, and 16 all with a ReLU activation function and a clamp on the output of 0 and 180 for min and max arm angles. The network has trained on labaled data of 0, 45, 90, 135, and 180 degrees across 30,000 data points. Data was collected by manually labeling a video of flexion frame by frame. The network was trained for 4000 epochs with MSE loss, learning rate of 0.001, weight decay = 0.0002, and early stopping. The neural network has an R2 of 0.85. This outperformed the original firmware algorithm.

    Firmware

    An NRF52833 chip was used to process the EMG signal. A SAADC channel was multithreaded to sample our signal at 5 kHz, where a continuous absolute integral is taken to approximate the EMG strength. Based on a callibration protocol that begins at the initiation of the firmware, this value is then linearly mapped to a desired motor position. PWM is then used to control the servo motor, but if necessary, an PID loop will be employed should we switch to using a DC motor.

    Circuit Design

    Before the EMG signal reaches the microcontroller, EMG signals were first pre-processed via an analog circuit that filters and amplifies our desired signal. Based off literature values and our empirical testing, we found that the majority of EMG frequencies fall between 0.5 Hz and 500 Hz. Our analog circuit first takes the differential measurement between two surface electrodes, which is then processed through a 60Hz notch filter to remove power artifacts. The signal is then passed into a bandpass filter to eliminate noise and motion artifacts, before being amplified to have a pk-pk of around 2 V and an offset of 1.5V

    Future Goals

    Starting fall, we hope to refine our prototype from a benchtop device to a wearable design. This includes 3 key steps. First, we need to refine the algorithm's precision by increasing EMG sensors, adding supplementary safety sensors like pressure sensors and accelerometers, and potentially integrate the ML algorithm onto the microcontroller. Second, hardware refinements need to be made, focusing on creating a flexible shell that can be worn by different users. Last, battery and power supplys need to be consolidated onto a single rechargeable, portable DC supply.
